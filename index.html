Agus Adventure - How Far Can You Aguuuu?

Agus Adventure is an endless 2D runner mobile video game created using the Unity game engine for iOS, Android, Blackberry 10, Windows Phone, and Amazon App Store. The game was created to understand how to learn the Unity game engine and familiarise with publishing mobile video games on multiple platforms. Agus Adventure adopts a one touch game mechanism allow people from all ages to easily pick up the game and play, making the game a more marketable product.

Previously the idea was to create a fully-fledged 3D mobile game for mobile devices. However after carrying research and analysis of the most popular mobile games across all mobile platforms key similarities were derived, which were used to enhance the chances of my first game being successful. The research highlighted than an mobile game must be simple to play, no use of on screen virtual D-pad and employ the use of cute cartoon characters such as Angry Birds/Flappy Bird to appeal to a wider audience. The following factors led to the game appealing to a much broader audience due to the simplicity and visuals of the game. The graphics were created using Adobe Photoshop CS6 and Adobe Illustrator CS6.

The game was released in July 2014 and has been featured on numerous websites and TV channels including Crackberry, AppsDoAndroid and the Peruvian TV channel Tec.TV. In order for Agus Adventure to be featured, promotional assets were created which consisted of daily posters, game trailers and slogans such as “How Far Can You Aguuuu?”

Agu Splash - How Far Can You Aguuuuu?

Agu Splash is the sequel to the 2D endless runner mobile game Agus Adventure. The game was published to a fewer number of platforms, due to the focus on social functionality for the game. The user would interact with the game by tilting the Agu alien left and right to climb on top of platforms and gain power-ups by eating cookies. Once the user finished their gameplay session, the user can share their gameplay footage alongside with their commentary using the in built microphone via Kamcord. The ability to share game footage allowed for greater marketing for the game, as the competition factor engaged most people to play the game and beat the score of their friends or family.

Agu Splash was released in November 2014, alongside with similar marketing material from the previous game, this included a game trailer, and daily updates on Twitter using the slogan “How Far Can You Aguuuu” and uploads from users around the world.

In addition, the game was designed to be played in portrait mode, as this prevents the user from tilting their phone, creating a larger footprint and making it less likely to be played one handed, reducing the number of casual users.

  • Platforms: iOS Android Blackberry 10

Mr Scoop - How Much Can You Scoop In 60s?

Mr Scoop is an iOS exclusive available on to the App Store. Mr Scoop has been designed from the ground up to appeal to all iOS devices users by integrating social and competitive elements. The aim of the game is to play one-handed and capture all the cookies within 60 seconds. Due to the fact that the a game session only lasts 60s and can be played with one hand the game will be adopted by casual gamers who are performing their daily activities such as taking public transport or waiting for someone.

The game increases in difficulty in the last ten seconds by increasing the amount of cookies falling down. Once the user has finished the game, their score can be shared on Facebook and Twitter highlighting their “Scoop Score” with the hashtag #mrscoop. The use of sharing score via social media, will allow the game to be more marketable as the game would act as a common interest with friends and family.

Mr Scoop has been submitted to the App Store and is currently waiting review, however marketing material have been utilised to create a buzz for the game. The marketing material includes mock-ups of the game with the catchphrase “How Much Can You Scoop In 60s? Mr Scoop Coming Soon To The @AppStore #mrscoop”. In additional promotional videos have been created lasting 15 seconds, to maintain the attention of the target audience as well as the ability to be shared on platforms such as Twitter and Vine due to only lasting 15 seconds.

  • Platform: iOS

LearnUrdu - E-Learning Application

LearnUrdu is a Built for Blackberry e-learning application created for the Blackberry 10 platform, which teaches users how to speak Urdu. The application aims to teach users Urdu by a combination of interactive exercises along with voice snippets, video tutorials and pictures to aid users in learning Urdu. The application was pitched in front of 200 developers and when completed gained 2nd Position with a reward of $2000 Canadian dollars.

LearnUrdu was created during the Blackberry Summer Camp Jam held in Madrid. The main focus of the event was to help developers create Built for Blackberry purposeful apps that would entice users to buy a Blackberry 10 handset over other rivals.

Initially the app idea was to create a video game using HTML5 WebWorks and the Construct 2 game engine to create a mobile video game. However, this had two major drawbacks, one was that the game would not be compatible across all mobile devices such as the Blackberry Q10 due to the aspect ratio. The second problem was that the mobile video game would not perform as efficiently as native application and would not incorporate the additional requirements that a Built for Blackberry application has, which was only awarded to application which signifies to customers the Blackberry 10 application delivers a signature Blackberry 10 experience.

When first arriving to Madrid, It was difficult to make conversation and communicate to the other 200 developers, which 90% were Spanish and English was not their first native language. This gave me the idea of making an e-learning language application, which taught users within UK how to speak Urdu due to the demand, as there are over 100 million people using Urdu as a first language with 40 million being purely within the UK. The application was also suited for the Blackberry 10 demographic due to their main target being enterprise and would suit businessmen and women who travel abroad frequently and find it difficult to interpret and understand language within certain part of Asia and Europe.
